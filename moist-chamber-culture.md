# Moist Chamber Culture of Myxomycetes

**Last updated:** June 2026  
**Reference:** Molina-Viramontes et al. (2025). https://doi.org/10.1111/jeu.70058  
**Adapted from:** Stephenson & Stempen (1994). *Myxomycetes: A Handbook of Slime Molds*. Timber Press.

---

## Purpose

Cultivation of myxomycete plasmodia from field-collected substrate under non-sterile, field-like conditions. Sporophores obtained by this method retain their native bacterial microbiota (**eubiosis state**) and are referred to as **Moist Chamber Sporocarps (MCSp)**.

---

## Materials

- Petri dishes (90 mm)
- Sterile filter paper discs
- Distilled water
- Digital pH meter (e.g., Hanna Instruments, RI, USA)
- Stereomicroscope (e.g., OLYMPUS SZX7)
- Inoculation loop
- Cardboard boxes (5 × 5 × 2 cm) for sporophore storage
- Optional supplements: oat flakes (*Avena sativa*), corn flour, sorghum

---

## Field Collection

1. Collect substrate (aerial litter, bark, or decaying wood) from natural populations of Physarales myxomycetes.
2. Record for each sample: collection number, GPS coordinates, date, substrate type, and collector.
3. Store in paper bags at room temperature; process within 48 h.

> **Example:** *Claustria didermoides* was isolated from aerial litter of *Senecio* sp. at National Park "La Malinche" (19°21.543′ N, 98°16.068′ W), Tlaxcala/Puebla, Mexico, on May 2, 2023.

---

## Protocol

### Setup

1. Place a sterile filter paper disc inside a Petri dish.
2. Add field-collected substrate on top of the filter paper.
3. Add distilled water until the substrate is fully saturated.
4. Close the dish and leave for **24 hours**.
5. After 24 h, measure the pH of the excess water with the digital pH meter, then drain it.

### Monitoring

6. Inspect chambers periodically (every 2–3 days) under the stereomicroscope for signs of myxomycetes: tracks, plasmodia, sclerotia, or fruiting bodies.
7. Continue monitoring for up to **3 months**, adding distilled water as needed to keep substrate moist.
8. If fungal mycelium or other contaminants appear, eliminate using a flame-heated inoculation loop.

### Harvesting

9. When mature sporophores are detected, harvest them with forceps and affix to a 5 × 5 × 2 cm cardboard box for morphological and molecular identification.
10. Deposit voucher specimens in an institutional herbarium (e.g., TLXM at UAT).

---

## Plasmodium Expansion (for biomass production)

To obtain sufficient plasmodium biomass for DNA extraction:

10. Cut sections of vigorous plasmodium (with substrate) using a sterile scalpel.
11. Transfer to new moist chambers with the original substrate ± supplements (oats, corn flour, or sorghum) to stimulate growth.
12. Repeat until abundant plasmodium is obtained.
13. Allow to sporulate to obtain MCSp under non-sterile conditions.

---

## Notes

- Record pH at setup; it provides useful baseline information about substrate chemistry.
- Supplements (especially oat flakes) significantly boost plasmodium growth and biomass.
- MCSp are appropriate controls for eubiosis state in microbiome studies.
- Flame-heated loops effectively control localized fungal contamination without disturbing the myxomycete.
